## Track to Generate

**Working title:** Machine Riot
**Genre:** 1980s synth-pop dance, big beat, electronic rave, and drum & bass influences
**Tempo:** fixed at 128 BPM
**Duration:** approximately 2 minutes
**Key:** E minor
**Time signature:** 4/4
**Mood:** energetic, aggressive, rhythmic, dynamic, urban, and slightly industrial

Generate an original, highly danceable track built around a powerful electronic four-on-the-floor kick on every quarter note.

The kick must be one of the dominant elements of the mix.

It must be:

* deep, heavy, and clearly audible;
* punchy, with a strong transient;
* present on every quarter note;
* powerful enough to drive the entire track;
* controlled in the sub range;
* distinct from the bass rather than masking it;
* stronger and fuller during the main sections and final climax.

The drum section must have a powerful electronic character, featuring:

* a deep and forceful four-on-the-floor kick;
* a prominent gated snare;
* electronic clap layers in selected sections;
* closed hi-hats in eighth- and sixteenth-note patterns;
* short open hi-hats on the offbeats;
* ghost snares and short drum & bass-inspired rhythmic fragments;
* frequent but controlled fills;
* retriggers and snare rolls before section changes;
* brief rhythmic pauses to make the following drop more impactful.

The bass must be one of the loudest and most important musical elements.

It must be clearly audible throughout the track and must remain present even during the busiest drum sections.

The bass sound must have:

* a strong and clearly defined fundamental;
* a full low-frequency body;
* audible second and third harmonics;
* a sharp and immediate attack;
* a slightly distorted or saturated electronic character;
* short and controlled notes;
* enough midrange content to remain audible on small speakers;
* a solid low end without becoming muddy.

The bass line must be:

* pulsing;
* syncopated;
* energetic;
* rhythmically active;
* varied between sections;
* tightly connected to the kick without being completely overlapped by it.

Use alternations between:

* root notes;
* fifths;
* octave jumps;
* short chromatic passing notes;
* repeated notes;
* occasional rests;
* offbeat accents.

The bass must not repeat the exact same pattern throughout the entire track.

Create different bass variations for:

* the intro;
* the main groove;
* the first development;
* the breakdown;
* the final return;
* the climax.

Use the following main harmonic progression:

`Em – C – G – D`

For darker transitions or breakdown sections, use:

`Em – D – C – B`

The chords should mainly appear as short, aggressive synthetic stabs rather than long sustained chords.

Add a mechanical electronic arpeggio based on the current harmony.

The arpeggio must be:

* precise;
* rhythmic;
* repetitive but not static;
* varied through note-order changes;
* occasionally transposed by one octave;
* interrupted by short rests;
* dynamically altered between sections.

Create a completely original main synth riff that is energetic, memorable, and rhythmically strong.

The riff should include:

* short notes;
* clear rests;
* rhythmic call-and-response phrases;
* small pitch slides;
* subtle vibrato;
* variations at the end of repeated phrases;
* a more intense version during the final section.

The riff must not play continuously.

Introduce it gradually, remove it during the breakdown, and bring it back with greater impact.

Suggested structure:

1. Intro with reduced kick, metallic percussion, and short bass fragments.
2. Full groove entrance with powerful kick, bass, and hi-hats.
3. Introduction of the main synth riff.
4. First variation with denser percussion.
5. High-energy main section.
6. Breakdown with reduced kick, fragmented bass, industrial atmosphere, and delayed notes.
7. Sudden full-groove return.
8. New bass and riff variation.
9. Final climax with fills, retriggers, arpeggio, lead, and the strongest kick and bass balance.
10. Short and controlled outro.

Use several tracker effects in a musical way, including:

* retrigger on percussion;
* note delay for ghost snares and echoes;
* note cut on bass notes and chord stabs;
* tone portamento on the lead;
* subtle vibrato;
* tremolo on sustained sounds;
* volume slides;
* fine pitch slides;
* sample offset on selected industrial hits;
* pattern delay in only one tension-building passage.

The final track must feel:

* highly rhythmic;
* powerful;
* dynamic;
* varied;
* aggressive but clear;
* rich in transitions;
* driven by a very strong kick;
* supported by a loud, full, and clearly audible bass;
* never static;
* never excessively repetitive.

The tempo must remain fixed at 128 BPM for the entire track.

All increases in intensity must come from rhythmic density, stronger drum patterns, bass variations, fills, effects, transitions, and arrangement development.
